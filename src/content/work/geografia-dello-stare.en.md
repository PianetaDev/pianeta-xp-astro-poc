## The project

*Geografia dello stare* — literally "the geography of dwelling" — is a **participatory inquiry** into how teenagers living in **Rome's Municipio III Montesacro** perceive well-being and unease across the neighborhood. It starts from one question — "how do girls and boys inhabit the territory they live in?" — and returns a map of the neighborhood written in their own words.

## The client

**Superabile** — an organization working on inclusion and rights — in partnership with **Municipio Roma III Montesacro**. The project is hosted on Pianeta.Green infrastructure with a custom editorial theme.

## Method

We collected quantitative data (who visits a place, how often, at what times) and qualitative testimonies (direct, first-person quotes, unfiltered). Results come back as a **scrollable editorial platform** alternating charts, maps, and the voices of the adolescents themselves.

## What emerges

The voices don't describe a place as *safe* or *unsafe* — they trace a subjective geography: some spaces are avoided for the group perceived to inhabit them, others for the time of day, others still — simply — because *"people are what makes me feel unsafe"*.
