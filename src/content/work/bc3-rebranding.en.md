## The starting point

The **Basque Centre for Climate Change** is one of the most recognized European research centers on climate change. It publishes peer-reviewed research, participates in Horizon Europe programs, and coordinates projects across research, policy, and outreach.

The previous identity could not hold together **three very different audiences** the center has to serve every day: the international scientific community, institutional decision-makers, and the general public (including schools, media, and local partners).

## The rebranding objective

Build a visual system that tells a story of **positive change, impact, responsibility** — the three concepts at the heart of the center's mission — without compromising scientific rigor and without falling into the "green corporate" aesthetic that already saturates the sector.

The identity had to be:

- **Recognizable** in academic publications as much as on social media
- **Adaptable** across more than 30 project sub-brands (including the later [UNTWIST](/work/untwist))
- **Sustainable** — including physically, in printed materials
- **Multilingual** (English · Spanish · Catalan · Basque)

## What we delivered

- **Logo** + system of variants (positive, negative, monochrome, small-format isolated)
- Complete **Brand Manual** with usage rules, palette, typography, grids, and icon system
- **Stationery** — letterhead, business cards, email signature templates, C5 envelope
- **Editorial templates** for papers, reports, executive summaries, policy briefs
- Modular **social kit** with a visual system for four different audiences
- **Sub-brand system** for related projects (template later applied to UNTWIST)

## The approach

**Co-design with BC3's communication manager.** Amelia Ochoa led the center's internal voice; Pianeta.Studio brought the visual system and art direction. Three iteration cycles, each with navigable prototypes applied to the center's real use cases.

**Validation on actual materials.** Every brand decision was tested on a real piece (a report, a LinkedIn post, a sample slide) before approval. No "logo looks great in PowerPoint" — only "will this template hold up across a year of publications?"

**Designed to survive turnover.** The brand manual is written so a new team member can apply it correctly within two days of onboarding. No implicit decisions, no templates left open to interpretation.

## Outcome

- Identity live since **November 2024**
- Applied across **all institutional communication** of the center (website, publications, social, event presence)
- The system became the foundation for **UNTWIST** (2025) and other related projects with no need for a redesign
- Adopted internally as the recurring standard
