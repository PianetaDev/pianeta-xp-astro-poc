## The objective

Make the **water-energy-food Nexus** understandable to very different audiences — children, adults, decision-makers — starting from the question Anna Sperotto poses: *"Have you ever wondered how water, energy, and food are connected?"*

Every daily decision — what you eat, how you use energy — pulls a thread that moves everything else. The design challenge was turning this invisible web into something **tangible and walkable**.

## The approach: knots and threads

We built a **unified visual storytelling system** based on the metaphor of **knots and threads**:

- The **knots** are the points of choice — a supermarket shelf, a light switch, a tap
- The **threads** are the invisible connections every knot carries with it
- Pull a knot, and you see threads moving in the distance: in an aquifer, a field, a power plant

The system works at multiple reading levels — from a simple explanation for children to advanced concepts for researchers — using the same visual vocabulary. It was adapted into **Italian, Spanish, and Catalan**.

## What we delivered

- **Main book** — illustrated, ~120 pages, narrative + infographic apparatus
- **Set of educational cards** — interactive learning for classrooms and workshops
- **Social kit** — posts, stories, short videos to extend the reach of the message
- Complementary **educational resources** for teachers
- **[Dedicated website](https://untwist.bc3research.org)** as the hub for all resources

## Embodied sustainability, not declared

The physical production of the book honors the message it carries:

- **Fedrigoni Arena recycled** paper
- **Low-impact inks**
- Print processes with certified chain of custody
- Materials designed for **long-lasting use** (no didactic obsolescence)

## Outcome

- Distribution across the BC3 network + EU partner networks
- Multilingual versions live (IT/ES/CA)
- UNTWIST site live: **[untwist.bc3research.org](https://untwist.bc3research.org)**
- The metaphorical framework was adopted by BC3 as a recurring institutional communication asset

## What we learned

When a scientific concept has 4–5 dimensions touching one another (water, energy, food, individual choices, global systems), design can **provide a language** before providing a solution. The right metaphor — knots and threads, here — saves hundreds of words of explanation and becomes **visual memory** that stays with the reader.

This is exactly the kind of work we'd like to do more of: serious scientific research that needs to speak to the world, without giving up complexity.
