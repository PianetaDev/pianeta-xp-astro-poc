## The challenge

The **ChildFund Alliance World Index** measures the level of **social inclusion of women and children** across countries. A complex, rich, dense dataset — capable of speaking to human-rights experts but at risk of remaining invisible to the people who live those rights every day.

The original report was a technical PDF. Our job: **turn it into a digital story** that worked for the expert audience **and** for those encountering the topic for the first time — without compromising the rigor of the data or the voice of those at the center: children.

## The objective

Design an **engaging narrative longform** that makes the World Index accessible to a global audience, putting the **voices of children** at the heart of the narrative — not as decoration.

## The approach: data storytelling with voice

Three guiding principles:

**1. Card-based structure.** We broke the longform down into **modular cards** that readers can move through at their own pace. One card = one idea, one metric, one story. No forced-scroll paywall.

**2. Illustration and collage.** To bring **warmth** to the data we paired infographic rigor with child-like illustrations and collage. Tones that recall a child's hand — not the generic NGO stock photo.

**3. Micro-interactions.** Light animations, narrative transitions, scroll-reactive elements. **A user experience that invites exploration**, not passive reading.

## What we delivered

- **Responsive digital longform** optimized for desktop and mobile
- A modular **narrative card system** (reusable in future editions)
- **Custom illustrations** + collage system
- **Micro-interactions and animations** tied to content
- A **navigation system** that lets readers control their own pace

## The outcome

- Perceived length reduced thanks to the card-based structure (active reading experience under 10 minutes)
- High message accessibility: non-specialist readers can grasp the meaning of the Index and read specific cases
- An editorial pattern **reusable for future editions of the World Index** without a redesign
- Adopted as the digital reporting standard of ChildFund Alliance + WeWorld
