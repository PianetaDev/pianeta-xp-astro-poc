Ludovica Ranzini is a designer and illustrator at Pianeta.Studio, an Italian Benefit Corporation operating at the intersection of sustainable design, technology, and social impact. She has collaborated with the studio since September 2025, working on custom illustration, data visualization, and UX/UI design. She studied at the University of Milano-Bicocca, with a Master's degree in Communication Theory and Technology and a Bachelor's degree in Psychosocial Sciences of Communication, and complemented her academic path with dedicated training in visual practice across illustration, lettering, and motion. Pianeta.Studio is her first structured professional experience in design and communication, building on the themes she explored throughout her studies.

At Pianeta.Studio, she handles the illustrative and visual component of projects, from data visualization to interface. Among her most significant works, she created the illustrations for the Food Reboot project and the cover illustration for the magazine of the Osservatorio delle Libere Professioni, worked on the Last APS landing page and the Missing Children project, and contributed to the build of websites and landing pages. Her central interest lies at the intersection of illustration and data visualization, in service of sustainability communication: visual languages able to hold together consistency, clarity of data, and narrative power.
